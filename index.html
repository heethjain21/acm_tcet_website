About Us

Curious about who we are?


The misson of ACM TCET has always been to articulate, promote, and dissemination of research related to the field of IT.

The chapter was founded in September 2011, which is managed by Department of Information Technology and currently has a total count of 550+ members.

The chapter offers seminars, lectures, and the opportunity to meet peers and experts in many fields of interest.

The chapter also organizes Educational Excustion (Industrial Visit) and the annual technical fest Zephyr.

What we do?

Vision

Serve both professional and public interests by fostering the open exchange of information.

Mission

Challenge and empower students to unleash their potential and contribute to the industry.

Goal

Enrich student experience by creating a collaborative community to achieve technical excellence and foster holistic development.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get Your doubts cleared!

  • How do I become a part of ACM TCET?

    To become an ACM TCET member, you have to activate the membership by paying a nominal fee.

  • What are the perks after becoming a member?

    ACM provides the members with various benefits like TechNews newsletter, webinars, discussion forums and many other benefits. Being an ACM TCET member provides you with the perks of attending ACM activities for discounted or no cost, and gives a preference to ACM members in case the seats are filled.

  • Can I join ACM even if I am not very technically sound?

    Yes, anyone can join ACM. ACM helps students grow and develop their technical as well as interpersonal skills.

  • What are some common chapter activities?

    Technical and non-technical workshops & games, coding competitions, hackathons, educational excursion/industrial visit, annual magazine are our common activities.
